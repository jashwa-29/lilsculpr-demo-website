Structured Syllabus

Hands-On Clay Modelling Learning

Clay modelling is a hands-on art form. Kids learn best when they practice regularly. Our gallery reflects how children improve step by step through our structured clay modelling syllabus. From simple shapes to detailed models, each image tells a learning story.

1

Simple Shapes to Foundation

Students begin with basic shapes like balls, cylinders, and cones. These foundational skills build confidence and hand coordination.

2

Combining Shapes & Creativity

Children learn to combine basic shapes into recognizable objects like fruits, animals, and miniature food items.

3

Adding Details & Refinement

Students develop fine motor skills by adding textures, patterns, and intricate details to their creations.

4

Advanced Mastery & Expression

Advanced students create complex sculptures, realistic figurines, and express their unique artistic vision with confidence.

From simple shapes to detailed models, each image tells a learning story of growth and achievement.
